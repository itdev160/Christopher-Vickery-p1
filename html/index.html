Formula report

2020 : Nurburgring - Germany

The Eifle Grand Prix and Nurburgring, Germany

Formula 1 moved on to Germany this week to take on a track not seen in the championship sense 2013. To add to the challenge of the Nurburgring cold weather and rain added acted to cool some spirits. On Friday heavy fog and rain grounded the medial helicopters causing the cancellation of both free practice one and two. The low clouds and rain cleared up on saturday, allowing both the morning practice session and qualifying take place without a hitch.

A week, and weekend of surprises off the race track.

This week brought Formula one some sad news. Honda, engin pattern to both Red Bull and Alphatauri has decided to leave the F1 family once again after the 2021 season. This comes as a shock to many with how well both teams have done this season. Honda claims that the decision only has to do with a push towards a more sustainable approach to their car design, but one can not help but think that the current world condition must have some effect. Either way Red Bull and its sister team are left to find a new engine supplier, or take up the rumored plan to buy faculties from Honda to continue to project.

Sickness struck for one driver this weekend. Racing Points' Lance Stroll found himself unable to take part in the festivities due to gastrointestinal distress. Nico Hulkenberg got the call up once again to fill the seat. Lucky living relatively close to the track meant a man who was literally drinking coffee with friends on saturday morning could get through the covid prescreening in time to make it to qualifying. The lack of practice sessions for him lead to a last place qualification, but considering the circumstances being less the one second off the back is commendable.

Race Day: cold with a chance of engin problems

Like much of the weekend Sunday proved a cold one, at 8° (nearly lowest temperature race on record) tire management proved to be a large problem for many drivers. The race proved eventful form the start with the mercedes trading places twice in the first lap. Daniel Ricciardo rapidly passed both Alex Albon and Charles Leclerc, which allowed him to inherit third when Valtteri Bottas ran into power unit troubles.

The drive with the worst luck for the day could probably be considered Albon. Forced into an early pitstop lead to him being near the back behind Dannill Kvyat. A poorly planed passing attempt lead to him clipping Kvyat front wing leading to a penalty and latter a pierced radiator forced an early withdrawal from the race. Lando Norris and Esteban Ocan also found them selves exiting early with hydraulic and power unit problems respectively, along with George Russell who's suspension broke after a run in with Kimi Raikkonen.

Lucky and History

The big winners this weekend were hands down Danile Ricciardo and Nico Hulkenberg. Hulkenbergs last to eight drive was a thing of beauty and shows that he deserves a full time seat once again. Renault had their best weekend in nearly a decade with Daniel Ricciardo delivering their first podium sense the team rejoin F1. But the biggest piece of history lands on the shoulders of Lewis Hamilton. Hamilton's 91st win ties him with Michael Schumacher for most wins in the championship. The achievement could be considered even more appropriate with the tieing takeing place at a track Schumacher won at 5 times. In a touching moment Mich Schumacher presented Lewis with a race helmet his farther had worn in a race at the Nurburgring. With another record in the books, let along the likening tieing of Schumachers championship winning record, Hamiltion has once again proven him self as one of the best drivers to ever grace a track.
